Question 241 :
Census town must have a density of population more than:

  1. 200 persons/sq. km      
  2. 300 persons/sq. km      
  3. 400 persons/sq. km      
  4. 500 persons/sq. km
  400 persons/sq. km  

Question 242 :
The Tropical Rain Forest or Equatorial type of climate is found in

  1. Amazon basin
  2. Congo basin
  3. Indonesian Islands
  4. All the above
   All the above

The Bedouins are inhabitants of _____.

  1. Australia
  2. Northern Canada
  3. The Arabian Desert
  4. Central Asia

Question 245 :
Among the following places, which one receives comparatively lowest average monsoon rainfall from June to September? [Asstt Comm 2008]

  1. Eastern Uttar Pradesh
  2. Southern West Bengal
  3. Punjab
  4. Western Uttar Pradesh

Question 246 :

  1. Danube
  2. Rhine
  3. Rhone
  4. Volga

Question 247 :
The main reason behind rapid growth of slums in India is:

  1. Economic deprivation
  2. Political Instability        
  3. Rural-urban migration    
  4. None of these
  Rural-urban migration

Question 248 :
How many circles encircles the CBD proposed in the concentric zone theory?

  1. 4            
  2. 3        
  3. 5            
  4. 6

Question 249 :
Indian monsoon is marked by seasonal shift caused by: [Teachers' Exam 1994]

  1. differential heating of land and sea
  2. cold winds of Central Asia
  3. great uniformity of temperature
  4. None of these
   differential heating of land and sea

Question 250 :
Which of the following is the most important factor in soil formation?

  1. Relief
  2. Climate
  3. Natural vegetation
  4. Rock cover
   Climate

Question 251 :

  1. Jhuggi-Jhompdi      
  2. Jhopadpatti      
  3. Ahatas  
  4. Bustees

Question 252 :

Question 253 :
In which area are Zoroastrians concentrated?

  1. Tamil Nadu
  2. Andhra Pradesh
  3. Maharashtra

Question 254 :
Xerophytic vegetation is characteristic feature of: [IAS 2004]

  1. Chhotanagpur plateau
  2. Khasi hills
  3. Eastern Ghats
  4. Kuchchh

Question 255 :
Which of the following statements is not true?

  1. The intensity of monsoons is determined by tropical depressions
  2. Tamil Nadu receives rainfall from north-east monsoon
  3. Andhra Pradesh is not much affected by retreating south-west monsoon
  4. All are correct
   All are correct

Question 256 :
Qualitative method is a scientific method of observation to gather:

  1. Non-numerical data      
  2. Numerical data      
  3. Both ( and (        
  4.     None of these
  Non-numerical data

Question 257 :
Use of statistical method for classifying towns is a method of:

  1. Qualitative method      
  2. Quantitative Method  
  3. Secondary method    
  4. None of these
  Quantitative method

Question 258 :
Which of the following are true in respect of alluvial soil ? 1. Generally confined to river basins 2. It has been deposited by rivers 3. It is rich in phosphorus and poor in potash 4. It is the most fertile soil

  1. I and II
  2. III and IV
  3. I, II and IV
  4. I, II and III
   I, II and IV

Question 259 :
Full form of BSUP is:

  2. Bharat Service Uchaya Privaka        
  3. Basic Service to Urban Poor      
  4. None of these

Question 260 :
Some people in Manipur live in houses bunt on floating islands of weeds and decaying vegetation held together by suspended slit. These islands are called

  1. Tipis
  2. Barkhans
  3. Phoomdis
  4. Izba
   Phoomdis

Question 261 :
Meaning of Suburb:

  1. The process of growth of urban population                        
  2. The study of the life of the city                                
  3. The movement of people out of cities to the surrounding areas                
  4. A residential district located on the outskirts of a city.
  A residential district located on the outskirts of a city.
