Question 1441 :
The Tenth Schedule of Indian Constitution deals with —

  1. Anti-defection Legislation
  2. Panchayati Raj
  3. Land Reforms
  4. Distribution of powers between the Union and States
  Anti-defection Legislation

Question 1442 :
The most important feature of the Indian Parliament is that:

  1. It is the union legislature in India
  2. It also comprises of the President
  3. It is bicameral in nature
  4. The upper house of the parliament is never dissolved
  It also comprises of the President

Question 1443 :
Which one of the following statements correctly describes the Fourth Schedule of the Constitution of India?

  1. It lists the distribution of powers between the Union and the States.
  2. It contains the languages listed in the Constitution.
  3. It contains the provisions regarding the administration of tribal areas.
  4. It allocates seats in the Council of States.
  It allocates seats in the Council of States.

Question 1444 :
The Government of India Act, 1935 was based on:

  1. the principle of federation and parliamentary system
  2. the principle of succession of the British Indian provinces
  3. acceptance of the idea of a Constituent Assembly to draft a constitution
  4. None of these
  the principle of federation and parliamentary system

Question 1445 :
When the Vice President officiates as President he draws the salary of

  1. Chairman of Rajya Sabha
  2. Both President - Vice President
  3. President of India
  4. Member of Parliament
  President of India

Question 1446 :

  1. Education
  2. Property

  4. Age

Question 1447 :

Question 1448 :
Five year plan in India is finally approved by _

Question 1449 :

Question 1450 :

  3. Ministers of State

Question 1451 :
In India, the Prime Minister remains in office so long as he enjoys the -

  1. Support of armed forces
  2. Confidence of Rajya Sabha
  3. Confidence of Lok Sabha
  4. Support of the people

Question 1452 :
The power to decide an election petition is vested in the

  1. High Court
  2. Election Commission
  3. Parliament
  4. Supreme Court
  Election Commission

Question 1453 :
India has Parliamentary Democracy because:

  1. Of distribution of power between the Centre and 0the States.
  2. The Council of Ministers is responsible to the 3Legislature.
  3. The Members of the Lok Sabha are elected by the public.
  4. Of a single constitutional framework.

Question 1454 :

  1. Vice- President
  2. President
  3. Home Ministry
  4. Cabinet Secretary

Question 1455 :

Question 1456 :
Who among the following was the first woman chief minister in independent India?

  1. Amrit Kaur

  3. Sucheta Kriplani

Question 1457 :

  1. Diet

  3. Yuan

Question 1458 :
The election disputes regarding the election of President and Vice —President can be heard by:

  1. Parliament
  2. Central Election commission
  3. Supreme Court
  4. Attorney-General of India
  Supreme Court

Question 1459 :
Despotism is possible in a:

  1. One party state
  2. Two party state
  3. Multi party state
  4. Two and multi party state
  One party state

Question 1460 :
In Indian Polity which one is Supreme?

  1. The Supreme court
  2. The Constitution
  3. The Parliament
  4. Religion
  The Constitution

Question 1461 :
What was the basis for constituting the Constituent Assembly of India?

  1. The resolution of Indian National Congress
  2. The Cabinet Mission Plan, 1946
  3. The Indian Independence Act, 1947
  4. The resolution of the Provincial / State legislature of the Dominion of India
  The Cabinet Mission Plan, 1946

Question 1462 :
Article 16 provides equally of opportunity in matters of public employment for:

  1. All citizens.
  2. All persons.
  3. All persons except aliens.
  4. Citizen as well as non-citizens.
  All citizens.

Question 1463 :
Point out which from the following is NOT a right enumerated in the Constitution of India but has been articulated by the Supreme Court to be a fundamental right?

  1. Right to life
  2. Equality of opportunity in matters of public employment
  3. Protection of personal liberty
  4. Right to permanent liberty
  Right to permanent liberty

Question 1464 :
