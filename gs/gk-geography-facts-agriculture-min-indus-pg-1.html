Question 1 :
The region which does not receive most of its rainfall during winter, is :

  1. Central California
  2. Central North America
  3. South West Australia
  4. South West Africa
   Central North America

Question 2 :
Economy of Brazil is mostly dependent on :

  1. tea
  2. coffee
  3. rice
  4. maize
   coffee

Question 3 :
A large number of species are found within a small unit of area of: [CDS 1999]

  1. mangrove coastal forests
  2. coniferous temperate forests
  3. deciduous monsoon forests
  4. wet evergreen equatorial forests
   wet evergreen equatorial forests

Question 4 :
Which one among the following covers the highest percentage of forest area in the world?

  1. Temperate Coniferous forests
  2. Temperate Deciduous forests
  3. Tropical Monsoon forests
  4. Tropical Rainforests
   Temperate Coniferous forests

Question 5 :
In which season is the frequency of tropical cyclones in the Bay of Bengal maximum?

  1. During autumn
  2. After summer
  3. During winter
  4. During monsoon
   After summer

Question 6 :
Which of the following statements about forests is not correct? [NDA 1991]

  1. Coniferous forests of high latitudes yield soft wood
  2. Between 2000 m and 3000 m elevation on the Himalayas grow deodar, chinar and walnut
  3. Forests in equatorial regions of India are less dense than the monsoon forests
  4. Large-scale exploitation of forests has caused soil erosion and repeated floods
   Forests in equatorial regions of India are less dense than the monsoon forests

Question 7 :
Summers in Western Europe are warmer than those in Eastern Europe because of: [CDS 2002]

  1. the influence of the warm ocean current
  2. the influence of bright sunshine
  3. the lower mountains terrain
  4. peninsular nature of the continent
   the influence of the warm ocean current

Question 11 :
What happens to tropical soils when the rain forest is cleared? [IAS 2004]

  1. The soil improves to the extent that soluble nutrients are no longer depleted by the trees
  2. There is little effect on the soil because of its depth of
  3. Clearing interrupts the critical nutrient cycle and leads to soil leaching
  4. The soils stabilise and become excellent for grain

Question 12 :
The tropical grasslands of Africa are known as:

  1. Steppes
  2. Savannas

Question 13 :
Consider the following ecosystems: 1. Taiga 2. Tropical evergreen 3. Tropical deciduous 4. Tundra The correct sequence in decreasing order of the albedo values of these ecosystem is: [IAS 2002]

  1. 1, 4, 3, 2
  2. 4, 1, 2, 3
  3. 4, 1, 3, 2
  4. 1, 4, 2, 3
   4, 1, 3, 2

Question 14 :
Which of the following statements is correct?

  1. Steppes are the temperate grasslands of South America
  2. Prairies are the temperate grasslands of North America
  3. Pampas are the temperate grasslands of South Australia
  4. Forests-with trees which do not shed their leaves seasonally are called deciduous forests
   Prairies are the temperate grasslands of North America

Question 15 :
Nights are cooler in the deserts than in the plains because: [CDS 2002]

  1. sand radiates heat more quickly than the earth
  2. the sky remains clear most of the time
  3. sand absorbs heat more quickly than the earth
  4. none of the above reasons
   sand radiates heat more quickly than the earth
