Question 1419 :

Question 1420 :

  1. August 15, 1947.
  2. January 26, 1950.

  4. January 30, 1948.

Question 1421 :
Panchayati Raj System was adopted to:

  1. Make people aware of politics.
  2. Decentralize the power of democracy.
  3. Educate the peasants.
  4. To introduce a form of local self-government at the village, block and district levels.

Question 1430 :

Question 1431 :

  4. Dr. Zakir Hussain

Question 1432 :
The term socialist used in the Preamble means a State whose basic policy is to

  1. prohibit concentration of wealth.
  2. distribute equitably the natural resources.
  3. prohibit concentration of wealth and uplift the living standards of people.
  4. prohibit concentration of wealth and distribute equitably the natural resources.

Question 1433 :
The directive principles of state policy are:

  1. justicable
  2. non-justicable
  3. Only some of the provisions are justicable
  4. None of the above
  non-justicable

Question 1434 :
The first attempt to introduce a representative and popular element in the governance of India was made through:

  1. Indian Council Acts, 1861
  2. Indian Council Acts, 1892
  3. Indian Council Acts, 1909
  4. Government of India Act, 1919

Question 1435 :
If a Minister loses a no- confidence motion, then

  1. Lok Sabha is dissolved
  2. Only Prime Minister and that Minister resigns.
  3. The whole Council of Ministers resigns
  4. The Minister resigns
  Lok Sabha is dissolved

Question 1436 :
In the event of a vacancy in the offices of both the President and vice-president of India, the person who performs the duty of a President is —

  1. Speaker of Lok Sabha
  2. Chief Justice of India
  3. Chairman of Rajya Sabha
  4. Attorney General
  Chief Justice of India

Question 1437 :
When can a President use his discretion for appointing the Prime Minister?

  1. When no political party enjoys a clear majority in the Lok Sabha.
  2. Only when the Lok Sabha has been dissolved.
  3. Under all circumstances
  4. President has no discretion

Question 1438 :
The preamble of our Constitution reads India as

  1. Sovereign, Socialist, Secular Democratic Republic

  3. Socialist, Sovereign, Democratic, Secular Republic
  4. Democratic, Sovereign, Secular, Socialist Republic
