Question 271 :
Which article of the constitution provides power to the parliament to make any provision with respect to acquisition and termination of citizenship and all other matters related to the citizenship:

  1. Article 11
  2. Article 13
  3. Article 12
  4. Article 111
  Article 11

Question 272 :
Which of the following writs/orders of the High court/supreme court is brought to get an order of an authority quashed?

  1. Mandamus
  2. Certiorari
  3. Quo Warranto
  4. Habeas Corpus
  Quo Warranto

Question 273 :
Article 1 of the Constitution of India declares India a Union of States. The territory of this Union of States comprises:

  1. Union and States.
  2. Union territories and States.
  3. States, Union territories, and States which may join the Union.
  4. States, Union territories and Territories which may be acquired.
  States, Union territories and Territories which may be acquired.

Question 274 :
The Indian Polity, the executive is subordinate to the —

  1. Judiciary
  2. Legislature
  3. Election commission
  4. Union Public Service Commission
  Legislature

Question 275 :
Under which article of the constitution of India can the President of India be impeached?

  1. Article 61
  2. Article 75
  3. Article 76
  4. Article 356
  Article 61

Question 276 :
Which of the following was the biggest source for the Constitution of India?

  1. The Government of India act 1919
  2. The Government of India act 1935
  3. The Indian independence act, 1947
  4. None of these

Question 277 :

  2. Dr. Radhakrishnan
  3. Zakir Hussain
  4. Neelam Sanjiva Reddy

Question 278 :
Among the following provisions, which one come into force soon after adoption of the Constitution on 26th November, 1949?

  1. Provisions relating to citizenship.

  3. Provisional Parliament

The vice-President is the ex-officio chairman of the-

Question 283 :
To elect the President of India, which one of the following election procedures is used?

  1. System of proportional representation by means of the single transferable vote
  2. Secondary voting system
  3. Proportional representation through list system
  4. Collective Voting system

Question 284 :
The High Courts in India were first started at

  1. Bombay, Delhi, Madras
  2. Madras and Bombay
  3. Bombay, Madras, Calcutta
  4. Delhi and Calcutta

Question 285 :
Who called Indian Federalism as the Co-operative Federalism?

  1. G. Austin
  2. K.C. Wheare
  3. Sir Ivor Jennings
  4. D.D. Basu

Question 286 :
Which of the following has been considered supreme as a source of power?

  1. Supreme Court of India.

Question 287 :
How many times a person of India can seek re-election for the post of President of India?

  1. Once

  3. 2 times
  4. Any number of times

Question 288 :
Although union list, state list and concurrent list cover the entire legislative business yet there may be an item not mentioned anywhere. Who will legislate on that item?

  1. Parliamentary only
  2. State legislature only
  3. Both a and b
  4. Neither a nor b

Question 290 :
By which of the following can the President of India be impeached?

  1. By the Lok Sabha
  2. By the parliament
  3. By the Chief Justice of India
  4. By the Prime Minister
  By the parliament

Question 291 :
In India, the Prime Minister remains in office so long as he enjoys the -

  1. Support of armed forces
  2. Confidence of Rajya Sabha
  3. Confidence of Lok Sabha
  4. Support of the people

Question 292 :
Consider the following statements — The objective of the Montague-Chelmsford Reforms of 1919 was: 1. To give power to the local government 2. To establish diarchy in the provinces 3. The extension of provincial government Which of these statements are correct?

  1. 1 and 2
  2. 1 and 3
  3. 2 and 3
  4. 1, 2 and 3
