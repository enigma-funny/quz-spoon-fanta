Question 391 :
With reference to the Revolt of 1857, who of the following laid siege to Jhansi in March 1858?

  1. Major General Wyndham
  2. Major General Havelock
  3. Sir Colin Campbell
  4. Sir Hugh Rose
   Sir Hugh Rose

Question 392 :
Which one of the following rebellions began in 1816 and lasted till 1832?

  1. Kol uprising
  2. Khasi uprising
  3. Kutch rebellion
  4. Naikada rising
   Kutch rebellion

Question 393 :
Firdausi was the Poet laureate at the court of —

  1. Mohd. Ghori
  2. Jaipal
  3. Mahmud Ghaznavi
  4. None of these
   Mahmud Ghaznavi

Question 394 :
Which one of the following pairs is correctly matched? Dynasties Rulers

  1. It meant dereliction of duty
  2. It was permitted only to the Kshatriyas
  3. It meant special sacrifices done Brahamanas for kings
  4. It meant duties permitted to different ‘varnas’ at times of distress
   It meant duties permitted to different ‘varnas’ at times of distress

Question 395 :

Question 397 :

  1. Surat, Calcutta, Bombay, Madras

  3. Madras, Calcutta, Bombay, Surat
  4. Calcutta, Madras, Surat, Bombay

Question 398 :

  2. 2 only
  3. Both 1 and 2

Question 402 :

  4. l, 3 and 4

Question 403 :

  3. 2600 kms
  4. 2700 kms

Question 404 :
Consider the following statements : 1) Lime mortar was never used at Mohenjo-daro in the construction of dwellings. 2) At Dholavira, stone was never used in theConstruction of dwellings. Which of the statement(s) given above is/are correct?

  1. 1,2,3,4
  2. 1,2,4,3
  3. 2,1,4,3
  4. 4 2 3 1

Question 405 :
Which was not one of the cardinal principles of Mahatma Gandhi’s doctrine of Satyagraha?

  1. Braham Gupta
  2. Varahmihira
  3. Vijayanandin
  4. Aryabhatta

Question 406 :
The pyramid of Gizeh was built by

  1. Athens
  2. Sparta
  3. Thebes
  4. Macedonia

Question 407 :
India for the Indians.' Who said?

  1. Swami Vivekanand
  2. Madan Mohan Malaviya
  3. Dayanand
  4. Bal Gangadhar Tilak

Question 408 :
Which one of the following events helped Gandhi who had returned from South Africa in 1915 more directly to emerge as the undisputed leader of the Indian National Congress ?

  1. He wanted to expedite the declaration of complete independence for India
  2. There was disidence in the Congress
  3. There was a communal riot
  4. He disapproved of the provision of separate electorates for the depressed classes in the British Prime Minister’s Communal Award
   He disapproved of the provision of separate electorates for the depressed classes in the British Prime Minister’s Communal Award

Question 409 :
The Non-co-operation Movement remained relatively weak in —

  1. Gold
  2. House
  3. Cattle wealth
  4. Land
   Cattle wealth

Question 410 :
‘Dipavamsa’ was written in—•

  1. Dasas
  2. Dasyus
  3. Mlecchas
  4. Yadus

Question 411 :
Mahatma Gandhi on August 8, ______.gave the call for Quit India Movement.

  1. 1942
  2. 1920
  3. 1930
  4. 1940
  1942

Question 412 :
Which one of the following regions is known for painting that are manuscript illustrations in miniature executed palm-leaf during the period A.D. 1100-1300?

  1. Vishnu Dharmashastra
  2. Manu smriti
  3. Yajnavalkya smriti
  4. Narada Smriti
   Manu smriti

Question 413 :
Ashoka’s invasion of Kalinga resulted in

  1. peaceful relations with Kalinga.
  2. the development of peace and non-violence as policy.
  3. wealth and prosperity Of Mauryan empire.
  4. victory to the policy of war and territorial conquests.
   peaceful relations with Kalinga.
