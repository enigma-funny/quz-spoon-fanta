Question 2431 :
The hottest planet is : [CPO SI 2002]

  1. Mercury
  2. Venus
  3. Jupiter
  4. Saturn
   Venus

Question 2432 :
What is the difference between asteroids and comets? 1. Asteroids are small rocky planetoids, while comets are formed of frozen gases held together by rocky and metallic material. 2. Asteroids are found mostly between the orbits of Jupiter and Mars, while comets are found mostly between Venus and Mercury. 3. Comets show a perceptible glowing tail, while asteroids do not. Which of the statements given above is/are correct? [CSAT 2011]

  1. 1 and 2 only
  2. 1 and 3 only
  3. 3 only
  4. 1, 2 and 3
   1 and 3 only

Question 2433 :
Which one of the following statements regarding Shooting Stars is incorrect ? [NDA 1995]

  1. They are meteors
  2. They are the debris which got separated from a comet
  3. They emit light due to extremely high temperature caused by friction while they are passing through earth's atmosphere
  4. They are a kind of a star
   They are a kind of a star

Question 2434 :
The strongest evidence that comets are members of our solar system comes from: [CDS 1992]

  1. their composition
  2. their effect on the lives of man
  3. the shape of their orbits
  4. the shape and length of their tails
   their composition

Question 2435 :
The sunspots cause: [UP PCS 1995]

  1. aurora borealis and aurora australis
  2. magnetic storms on the surface of the sun
  3. polar auroras
  4. all of these
   all of these

Question 2436 :

  1. 1 only
  2. 2 only
  3. Both 1 and 2
  4. Neither 1 nor 2

Question 2437 :

  1. 1, 2 and 3
  2. 1 and 3 only
  3. 2 only
  4. 1 and 2 only

Question 2438 :

  2. 1, 2, 3 and 4
  3. 2 and 4

Question 2442 :
The orbits of planets around the Sun, or of satellites around the Earth, can be : [IFS 1993]

  1. circular and elliptic
  2. circular and hyperbolic
  3. elliptic and parabolic
  4. parabolic and hyperbolic
   circular and elliptic

Question 2443 :
The correct sequence of planets in the descending order of their equatorial diameter is :

  1. Mars, Mercury, Uranus, Earth
  2. Earth, Mars, Mercury, Uranus
  3. Mercury, Uranus, Mars, Earth
  4. Uranus, Earth, Mars, Mercury
   Uranus, Earth, Mars, Mercury

Question 2444 :
Among the following which planet takes maximum time for one revolution around the Sun? [IAS 2003]

  1. Earth
  2. Jupiter
  3. Mars
  4. Venus
   Jupiter

Question 2445 :
A one-day Cricket match between India and England starts at 10.00 A.M. in London. The direct telecast of the match will begin in India at: [IFS 1993, CDS 1992]

  1. 3.30 A.M.
  2. 8.30 A.M.
  3. 3.30 P.M.
  4. 8.30 P.M.
   3.30 P.M.

Question 2446 :
Albedo is defined as:

  1. UV radiations sent out by the SUN
  2. a substance contained in the upper atmospheric layers of the earth, which is responsible for the reflection of/a part of solar radiations
  3. celestial bodies in earth's atmosphere which add to the greenhouse effect
  4. the amount of insolation reflected back to the space by the top of atmosphere, by clouds and ice-covered areas of the Earth's surface
   the amount of insolation reflected back to the space by the top of atmosphere, by clouds and ice-covered areas of the Earth's surface

Question 2451 :

  3. a group of stars
  4. none of these

  1. 2, 3, 1
  2. 1, 2, 3
  3. 3, 2, 1

The radiant energy of the sun is transmitted in the form of:
