Question 91 :
Western Gateway to the United States is

  1. Seattle
  2. San Diego
  3. San Francisco
  4. Los Angeles
   San Francisco

Question 92 :
The 38th parallel separates

  1. North Vietnam from South Vietnam
  2. South Korea from North Korea
  3. Hungary from Serbia
  4. Afghanistan from Uzbekistan
   South Korea from North Korea

Question 93 :
Plains formed by the deposits brought by glacier are

  1. lacustrine plains
  2. flood plains
  3. drift plains
  4. coastal plains
   drift plains

Question 94 :
Trincomalee is a port situated on the

  1. east coast, Tamil Nadu
  2. east coast of Sri Lanka
  3. east coast, Andhra Pradesh
  4. west coast of Sri Lanka
   east coast of Sri Lanka

Question 95 :
The 90º East Ridge lies in the _____Ocean.

  1. Pacific
  2. Atlantic
  3. Indian
  4. Arctic
   Indian

Question 96 :
Limestone changes into _____ due to metamorphism.

  1. marble
  2. quartzite
  3. gneiss
  4. schists

Question 97 :
Venus has atmosphere made up of

  1. Ammonia
  2. Carbon-di-Oxide
  3. Helium
  4. Hydrogen

Question 98 :
The largest fresh water body (volume-wise) in the world is lake

  1. Baikal
  2. Titicaca
  3. Superior
  4. Victoria
   Superior

Question 99 :
Carbonaceous rocks which produce coal and of belong to the category of rocks called

  1. metamorphic
  2. igneous
  3. sedimentary
  4. inorganic
   sedimentary

Question 100 :
Imaginary lines connecting places in oceans having equal salinity are called

  1. Isotherms
  2. Isobars
  3. Isohalines
  4. Isohyets
   Isohalines

Question 101 :
Which or the following is a rift valley?

  1. The Amazon Valley
  2. The Thames Valley
  3. The Rhine Valley
  4. The Mississippi Valley
   The Rhine Valley

Question 102 :
A strait is

  1. a narrow stretch of sea connecting two extensive areas of sea
  2. a narrow strip of land joining two large land-masses
  3. a wide indentation into the land formed by a sea or a lake
  4. a narrow headland
   a narrow stretch of sea connecting two extensive areas of sea

Question 103 :
Cut off loops of rivers are known as

  1. Bajada
  2. Meanders
  3. Oxbow lake
  4. Levee
   Oxbow lake

Question 104 :
The urban population of the world is growing faster than Its rural population. This is mainly because of

  1. high birth rate in towns
  2. rural-urban migration
  3. high death rate in rural areas
  4. low death rate in towns
   rural-urban migration

Question 105 :
Name the country which shares the benefits of the Kosi Multipurpose Project with India

  1. Bangladesh
  2. Pakistan
  3. Nepal
  4. Bhutan
   Nepal

Question 106 :
The total length of the oceanic ridge systems is of the order of

  1. 40000 km
  2. 65000 km
  3. 95000 km
  4. 150000 km
   65000 km

Question 107 :
Jute requires

  1. hot and humid climate
  2. cold climate
  3. hot and dry climate
  4. Mediterranean climate
   hot and humid climate

Question 108 :
The countries which are called the 'Rice Bowl' of Asia are

  1. China, Japan, Sri Lanka, India
  2. Myanmar, Thailand, Kampuchea and Vietnam
  3. India, Pakistan, Nepal, Mongolia
  4. Japan, Indonesia, Malaysia, India
   Myanmar, Thailand, Kampuchea and Vietnam

Question 109 :
Consider the following statements: 1. Ocean currents are the slow-surface movement of water in the ocean 2. Ocean currents assist in maintaining the Earth's heat balance 3. Ocean currents are set in motion primarily by prevailing winds 4. Ocean current are affected by the configuration of the ocean Which of these statements are correct?

  1. 1 and 2
  2. 2, 3 and 4
  3. 1, 3 and 4
  4. 1, 2, 3 and 4
   1, 2, 3 and 4

Question 110 :
Magma that reaches earth's surface and then solidifies is called

  1. granite
  2. lava
  3. quartz
  4. silicates
   lava

Question 111 :
Dally weather map showing isobars is an example of

  1. Choropleth map
  2. Isopleth map
  3. Chorochromatic map
  4. Choroschematic map

Question 112 :
Ladang refers to

  1. Shifting cultivation

  3. Subsistence type of agriculture

Question 113 :
