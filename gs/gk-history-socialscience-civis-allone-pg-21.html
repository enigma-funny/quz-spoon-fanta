Question 601 :
What was the worst part of the permanent settlement of Bengal?

  1. Disintegration of the village communities
  2. Creation of absentee landlords

  4. Subjection of the cultivator to untold misery

Question 605 :
Which one of the following events helped Gandhi who had returned from South Africa in 1915 more directly to emerge as the undisputed leader of the Indian National Congress?

  1. Edwin Montagu’s announcement on 20 August, 1917 in the House of Commons that the policy of the British Government would be the gradual development of self governing institutions with a view to the progressive realization of responsible government in India
  2. Annie Besant became almost overnight a pro-Raj supporter after Montagu’s promise of responsible government in India
  3. B. G. Tilak departed for England in September 1918 to fight a libel suit against Velentine Cherol
  4. The British Raj passed the Rowlett Act on 18 March, 1919 to suppress the revolutionary movements against it
   The British Raj passed the Rowlett Act on 18 March, 1919 to suppress the revolutionary movements against it
