Question 451 :
Grand Bank, Sable Bank, Middle Bank and Jeffery Bank are the major fishing grounds of

  1. North West Atlantic
  2. North East Pacific
  3. North East Atlantic
  4. North West Pacific
   North West Atlantic

Question 452 :
The biggest freshwater lake is

  1. Titicaca
  2. Baikal
  3. Superior
  4. Victoria
   Superior

Question 453 :
Shifting agriculture is most commonly practised in

  1. forests
  2. deserts
  3. plain areas
  4. grass lands
   forests

Question 454 :
The first oil well in the world was drilled at

  1. Assam
  2. Japan
  3. Pensylvania
  4. Former USSR
   Pensylvania

Question 455 :
_____ grasses are known as elephant grasses.

  1. Prairie
  2. Steppe
  3. Savannah
  4. Llanos
   Savannah

Question 456 :
The correct sequence of different layers of the atmosphere from the surface of the Earth upwards is:

  1. Troposphere, Stratosphere, Ionosphere, Mesosphere
  2. Stratosphere, Troposphere, Ionosphere, Mesosphere
  3. Troposphere, Stratosphere, Mesosphere, Ionosphere
  4. Stratosphere, Troposphere, Mesosphere, Ionosphere
   Troposphere, Stratosphere, Mesosphere, Ionosphere

Question 457 :
The only land-locked country in South-East Asia is

  1. Laos
  2. Thailand
  3. Malaysia
  4. Kampuchea
   Laos

Question 458 :
A landform composed of sediments brought by a river and found where it enters a main valley or emerges from a mountain on to a plain is called a/an

  1. meander
  2. alluvial fan
  3. oxbow lake
  4. levee
   alluvial fan

Question 459 :
Which of the following is not one of the three processes involved in the transfer of water between the atmosphere and the surface of the Earth?

  1. Insolation
  2. Evaporation
  3. Condensation
  4. Precipitation
   Insolation

Question 460 :
Wind erosion is dominant in

  1. Tropical regions
  2. Temperate regions
  3. Deserts
  4. Mediterranean regions

Question 461 :
International date line is a/an

  1. straight line
  2. curved line
  3. zigzag line
  4. elliptical line

Question 462 :
Valparaiso is an important port of

  1. Argentina
  2. Uruguay
  3. Chile
  4. Paraguay
   Chile

Question 463 :
Snow is formed by the process of

  1. condensation
  2. evaporation
  3. sublimation
  4. evaporation and condensation
   sublimation

Question 464 :
Along the coasts salinity is

  1. lowered

Question 465 :

  1. France

Question 466 :

  1. Uranus
  2. Neptune
  3. Saturn
  4. Mars

Question 467 :
World Environmental day is celebrated on ______ every year.

  1. May 1
  2. June 5
  3. October 24
  4. December 25

Question 468 :

  1. The Nile
  2. The Niger
  3. The Congo
  4. The Zambezi
   The Zambezi

Question 469 :
The amount of dew is measured by

  1. Pluviometer
  2. Drosometer
  3. Anemometer
  4. Hygrometer
   Drosometer

Question 470 :
The majority or the coal reserves found in India are of

  1. Tertiary formation
  2. Gondwana formation
  3. Quaternary formation
  4. Jurassic formation
   Gondwana formation

Question 471 :
Podsol is a type of soil which is characteristic of a region under the cover of ____forest.

  1. deciduous
  2. coniferous
  3. evergreen
  4. alpine

Question 472 :
The Great Circle routes join

  1. London to Los Angeles and Amsterdam to Tokyo
  2. Vancouver to Quebec and Perth to Melbourne
  3. Perth to Tokyo and London to Quebec
  4. Los Angeles to Perth and Tokyo to Bombay

Question 473 :

  1. core

  1. Volcanic mountains

  1. blond

Question 478 :
What is a graticule?

  1. The network of intersecting parallels and meridians on a map

Question 480 :
Selvas is a word denoting
