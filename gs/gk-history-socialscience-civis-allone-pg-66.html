Question 1951 :
Svayamvara was a special form of —

  1. Pahallavas
  2. Cholas
  3. Kadambas
  4. Vijaynagar

Which among the following is not an endogamous group?

  1. Caste
  2. Varna
  3. Gotra
  4. Religion
  Gotra

  2. Fakruddin Ali Ahmed
  3. EMS
  4. 1966

Question 1954 :
The earliest literate in India was in Sanskrit— it was learnt by

  1. Rama and Krishna
  2. Vishnu and Siva
  3. Brahma and Sun
  4. Brahma and Ganesha

Question 1955 :
The Mesopotamian society was divided into

  1. Between two rivers
  2. Between two oceans
  3. Between two mountains
  4. Between two islands

Question 1956 :

  1. 1936
  2. 1946
  3. 1956
  4. 1950
  1950

  4. 1585

Question 1963 :
On the ruins of which one of the following dynasties was the Chola empire established?

  2. Pahallavas of Kanchi
  3. Pandyas of Madurai
  4. Chalukyas of Kalyani

Question 1964 :
The contemporary source that provides satisfitical data immensely valuable to the study of economy of the Mughal Empire is the —

  1. Tabaqat-i-Akbari
  2. Ain-i-Akbari
  3. Tuhfah-i-Akbarshahi
  4. Badshahnama

During the period of Harshavardhana the fields were watered by the pots of the Ghatiyantra.' This is known from the writings of—

  1. Abul Hasan
  2. Mansur
  3. Bichitr
  4. Bishandas

Question 1966 :
Who condemned Gandhism as a reactionary social philosophy?

  1. Gopalakrishna Gokhale

  4. Lohia

Question 1967 :
Prehistoric axes are found at—

  1. 1200—1000 B.C.

  3. 2500 B.C.
  4. 1000 BC.

Question 1968 :
The Luck now Pact of 1916 was made between

  1. The moderates and extremists
  2. The British and the Indians
  3. the Hindus and the Muslims
  4. The Congress and the Muslim League
   The Congress and the Muslim League

Question 1969 :
The French Queen, Marie Antonitte, was termed as

  1. empty headed
  2. hot headed
  3. high headed
  4. dumb headed
   empty headed

Question 1970 :
Which of the following is correct?

  1. In the Chola kingdom the royal revenue from the peasants was collected by the officials appointed by the central govt.
  2. In the Chola kingdom the royal revenue from the peasants was collected by the village sabha
  3. In the Chola kingdom the royal revenue from the peasants was collected by the officials appointed by the district government
  4. In the Chola kingdom the royal revenue from the peasants was collected by the zamindars
   In the Chola kingdom the royal revenue from the peasants was collected by the village sabha

  2. Jahangir
  3. Shajahan
  4. Aurangzeb

  1. Hinduism
