  1. 1 only
  2. 1 and 3 only
  3. 2 and 3 only
  4. 1, 2 and 3

Question 1630 :
The President may for violation of the Constitution be removed from the office by

  1. The Prime Minister

  3. Process of Impeachment
  4. A vote of no- confidence vote

Question 1631 :
Indian Constitution permits discrimination on the basis of:

  1. Sex only.
  2. Caste and sex only.
  3. Caste, sex and place of birth.
  4. Not on any ground.

Question 1632 :
The right to 'equality before the law' contained in Article 14 of the Constitution of India is available to:

  1. Natural persons only.
  2. Legal persons only.
  3. Citizens of India.
  4. All persons whether natural or legal.

Question 1635 :
A law, which abridges Fundamental Rights, is not a nullity but remains inoperative till the shadow of Fundamental Rights falls over such rights. This is known as the doctrine of:

  1. Pleasure.
  2. Pith and substance.
  3. Eclipse.
  4. Severability.

Question 1637 :

  3. Four or more states

Question 1638 :

Question 1639 :
Dealing with the Chapter of Citizenship under the Constitution, the Supreme Court has held that Sonia Gandhi, the President of Congress is not an Italian citizen, but is an Indian citizen because she is governed by:

  1. Article 10.
  2. Article 11.
  3. The Citizenship Act, 1955.
  4. None of the above.
  The Citizenship Act, 1955.

Question 1640 :
Which one of the following is in correct order in the Preamble of the Indian Constitution?

  1. Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic.
  2. Sovereign, Secular, Socialist, Democratic, Republic.
  3. Sovereign, Secular, Democratic, Secular, Republic.
  4. Sovereign, Secular, Democratic, Socialist, Republic.
