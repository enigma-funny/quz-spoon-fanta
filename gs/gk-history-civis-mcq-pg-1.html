Question 1 :
The Council of Ministers of Indian Union is collectively responsible to —

  1. President
  2. Prime Minister
  3. Rajya Sabha
  4. Lok Sabha
  Lok Sabha

Question 2 :
Which of the following non-member of Parliament has the right to address it?

  1. Attorney General of India
  2. Solicitor General of India
  3. Chief Justice of India
  4. Chief Election Commissioner
  Attorney General of India

Question 3 :
All the following are fundamental rights guaranteed by the Indian Constitution except

  1. Right to equality
  2. Right against exploitation
  3. Right to adequate means of livelihood
  4. None of these
  Right to adequate means of livelihood

Question 4 :
The Finance Commission is constituted underArticle .... of the Constitution of India?

  1. 275
  2. 280
  3. 282
  4. None of these
  280

Question 5 :
As per the Constitution of India, if a President dies while in office, the Vice President can act as President for a maximum period of

  1. 1 years
  2. 3 months
  3. 6 months
  4. 2 years
  6 months

Question 6 :
Which one of the following is the mandatory minimum requirement for a functional federation?

  1. States should be represented in the Upper House of the National Legislature.
  2. Judicial Review.
  3. Distribution of legislative and administrative powers between the two sets of Governments, National and State, so as to ensure the Independence of the Governments within their respective spheres.
  4. Distribution of legislative and administrative power between the two sets of Governments, National and State, to ensure independent but coordinated exercise of powers of the Government.
  Distribution of legislative and administrative powers between the two sets of Governments, National and State, so as to ensure the Independence of the Governments within their respective spheres.

Question 7 :
The Rajya Sabha is dissolved after —

  1. Every five years
  2. Every six years
  3. On the advice of the Prime minister
  4. None of these
  None of these

Question 8 :
The Constitution of India was adopted by the Constituent Assembly of India on —

  1. 26th January, 1950
  2. 26th January, 1949
  3. 26th November, 1949
  4. 15th August, 1947
  26th November, 1949

Question 9 :
`Liberty, Equality and Fraternity' is a slogan associated with

  1. American war of Independence.
  2. Chinese Revolution.
  3. French Revolution.
  4. Russian Revolution.
  French Revolution.

Question 10 :
Which of the following is not an essential qualification for appointment as a Governor?

  1. He must not be a member of either House of Parliament.
  2. He should be a domicile of the state to which he is being appointed.
  3. He should be a citizen of India.
  4. He must have completed the age of 35 years
  He should be a domicile of the state to which he is being appointed.

Question 11 :
Who among the following enjoys the rank equivalent to the Cabinet Minister of the Indian Union?

  1. Secretary to the Government of India
  2. Deputy Chairman of the Planning Commission
  3. Deputy Chairman, Rajya Sabha
  4. None of the Above
  Deputy Chairman of the Planning Commission

Question 12 :
The 73rd Constitution Amendment Act, 1992 refers to the:

  1. Generation of gainful employment for the unemployed and the under-employed men and women in rural area.
  2. Generation of employment for the able bodied adults who are in need and desirous of work during the lean agricultural season.
  3. Laying the foundation for strong and vibrant Panchayati Raj Institutions in the country.
  4. Guarantee of right to life, liberty and security of person, equality before law and equal protection without discrimination.
  Laying the foundation for strong and vibrant Panchayati Raj Institutions in the country.

Question 13 :
How Many seats are reserved in all Panchayats at all levels for women?

  1. One-fourth.
  2. One-third.
  3. One-half.
  4. None of the above.
  One-third.

Which one of the following is the first state to have been formed on linguistic basis?

  1. Andhra Pradesh
  2. Gujarat
  3. Karnataka
  4. Punjab
  Andhra Pradesh

Question 15 :
How many times has financial emergency been declared in India so far?

  1. Once
  2. 4 times
  3. 5 times
  4. Never
  Never

Question 16 :
At which of its session, did the Congress officially accept the Socialistic Pattern of Society?

  1. Bombay Session of 1953
  2. Avadi Session of 1955
  3. Calcutta Session of 1957
  4. Nagpur Session of 1959

Question 17 :
wrong Consider the following Acts:

  1. The Regulating Act, 1773
  2. Pitt's India Act, 1784
  3. The Charter Act, 1793
  4. Indian Council Act,1861

Question 18 :
Provisions regarding Municpalities and Panchayats was made in the Indian Constitution in which year?

  1. 1991
  2. 1993
  3. 1995
  4. 2000
  1993

Question 19 :
The Union Executive of India consists of —

  1. The President, Vice-President and the Council of Ministers only
  2. The President and council of Ministers only
  3. The President, Vice president council of Ministers and Lok Sabha
  4. The President, Prime Minister, Deputy Chairman of Rajya Sabha, Lok Sabha speaker
  The President, Vice-President and the Council of Ministers only

Question 20 :
Which is National Flower of India?

  1. Rose
  2. Lotus
  3. Lily
  4. Sunflower
  Lotus

Question 21 :
The speaker of Lok Sabha can resign his office by addressing his resignation to —

  1. The president
  2. The prime Minister
  3. The Deputy Speaker of the Lok Sabha
  4. The chief justice of India
  The Deputy Speaker of the Lok Sabha

Question 22 :
Which of the following service is not part of the All India Services?

  1. LAS (Indian Administrative Services)
  2. IFS (Indian Forest Services)
  3. IPS (Indian Police Services)
  4. IMS (Indian Military Services)

Question 23 :
Who was the first Deputy Prime Minster of India?

Question 27 :
Article 78 of the Constitution deals with

  1. President's power to get information from the Council of Ministers.
  2. Prime Minister's duty regarding keeping the President informed about the government's decisions and policies.
  3. Emergency powers of the President

Question 28 :
The Governor of a state in India is —

  1. Directly elected by the people of India
  2. Appointed by the state chief Minister
  3. Appointed by the Prime Minister
  4. Appointed by the President
