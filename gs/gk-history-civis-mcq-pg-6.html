Question 151 :
Five year plan in India is finally approved by _

  1. Union Cabinet
  2. President on the advice of Prime Minister
  3. Planning Commission
  4. National Development Council
  National Development Council

Question 152 :
The President of India is elected by the —

  1. Members of the Lok Sabha
  2. Members of Both Houses of the Parliament
  3. Members of the state legislature
  4. By an electoral college consisting of the elected members of both Houses of the Parliament and state Assemblies.
  By an electoral college consisting of the elected members of both Houses of the Parliament and state Assemblies.

Question 153 :
The Chief Minister is appointed by —

  1. The Governor
  2. The President
  3. The Chief Justice of Supreme Court
  4. The Chief justice of High court
  The Governor

Question 154 :
The power to curve out a new state is vested in -

  1. The Parliament
  2. The President
  3. The Council of Ministers
  4. State's reorganization Commission
  The Parliament

Question 155 :
Ultimate sovereignty lies in:

  1. Parliament.
  2. President.
  3. Supreme Court.
  4. People.
  People.

Question 156 :
The Council of Ministers in a Parliamentary type of Government can remain in office till it enjoys the support of the

  1. Majority of members of both Houses of Parliament
  2. Majority of the members of the Upper House of Parliament
  3. Two third (2/ of members of the Lower House
  4. Majority of the members of the Lower House of Parliament
  Majority of the members of the Lower House of Parliament

Question 157 :

  1. there are six.
  2. there are five.
  3. there are eight.
  4. they have been regrouped in three categories-social, economic and political.

  1. The Prime Minister
  2. The Electoral College consisting of members of Parliament and the State Legislatures.
  3. Process of Impeachment

Question 161 :
Who was the Chairman of the Provincial Constitution Committee of the Constituent Assembly?

  1. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar
  2. Pt. Jawahar Lal Nehru

Question 162 :
The President can proclaim a state of emergency in case of

  1. Threat to financial stability of the country.
  2. External aggression or internal disturbances threatening the security of the country.
  3. Failure of Constitutional machinery in a particular state.
  4. All of the above

Question 163 :
Despotism is possible in a:

  1. One party state
  2. Two party state
  3. Multi party state
  4. Two and multi party state

Question 164 :

  1. Provisions relating to citizenship.
  2. Elections.
  3. Provisional Parliament
  4. All of the above
  All of the above

Under which article of the constitution of India can the President of India be impeached?

  2. Article 75
  3. Article 76

Article 1 of the constitution declares India as —

  2. Quasi-Federal State

Question 170 :

  1. Two years
  2. One year
  3. Six months
  4. Three years

Question 171 :
The annual report of the UPSC is submitted to:

  1. The President
  2. The Supreme Court
  3. The Prime Minister
  4. The Chairman of the Union Public Service Commission

Question 172 :
The Eighth Schedule of Indian Constitution contains the:

  1. Fundamental Rights.
  2. Directive Principles.
  3. A list of languages recognized by the Constitution.
  4. Number of States and Union Territories.

Question 173 :
How many types of Emergency have been visualised in the Constitution of India?

  1. Four

  3. One
  4. Two

Question 174 :
Fiscal Deficit is —

  1. Total Expenditures — Total receipts
  2. Revenue expenditures — Revenue receipts
  3. Capital expenditure — capital receipts — Borrowings

Question 175 :
The charge of impeachment against the President of India for his removal can be preferred by

  1. Both Houses of Parliament
