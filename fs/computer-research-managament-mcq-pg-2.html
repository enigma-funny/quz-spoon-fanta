Question 31 :
In a systematic sampling, if samples are to be drawn as 44 from 1000 elements then what will be the width of interval?

  1. 22.72
  2. 23
  3. 22
  4. 22.727
  23

Question 32 :
The measures of central tendency are

  1. Mean, mode, median
  2. Mean, mode, standard deviation
  3. Mean, standard deviation, variance
  4. Mean, variance, median
  Mean, mode, median

Question 33 :
Hypothesis refers to ____-

  1. The outcome of an experiment
  2. A conclusion drawn from an experiment
  3. A form of bias in which the subject tries to outguess the experimenter
  4. A tentative statement about the relationship
  A tentative statement about the relationship

Question 34 :
A census is preferred over sampling when

  1. The size of the population is very small.
  2. The size of the population is very big.
  3. There is a less variation in the population units.
  4. Most of the elements in the population are identical.
  The size of the population is very small.

Question 35 :
The objective of research methodology is to determine the frequency with which something occurs or with which it is associated with something else is called…..

  1. Formulative
  2. Descriptive
  3. Diagnostic
  4. Hypothesis testing
  Diagnostic

Question 36 :
Which of the following can be considered as evaluation research

  1. What are we doing?
  2. How well are we doing?
  3. Why are we doing?
  4. For whom we are doing?
  How well are we doing?

Question 37 :

Question 38 :
The formulated problem should be

  1. Opaque
  2. Factual

Question 39 :
A problem precisely defined

  1. cannot be solved
  2. is half- solved
  3. is Fully solved
  4. is difficult to solve

Question 40 :
Writing in _________ research is more descriptive and narrative than analytical, hence you need to use your imagination in terms of placement of information, linkage between the thoughts and flow of language to make the writing interesting to read and meaningful in conveying the findings.

  1. Quantitative
  2. Qualitative
  3. Technical
  4. Methodological

Question 41 :
Which of the following is not a subsidiary part of a formal report?

  1. Table of contents
  2. References
  3. Appendix
  4. Glossary
  Appendix

Question 42 :
The measure that identify the single numerical value as representative of an entire distribution are known as:

  1. Measure of central tendency
  2. Measure of dispersion
  3. Measure of shape
  4. Measure of association
  Measure of central tendency

Question 43 :
In ______________, the researcher has to use facts or information already available, and analyze these to make a critical evaluation of the material

  1. Experimental research
  2. Analytical research
  3. Quantitative research
  4. Descriptive research
  Analytical research

Question 44 :
Which experimental research design allows for statistical control of two non-interacting extraneous variables in addition to the manipulation of treatment variable?

  1. Completely randomized design
  2. Randomized block design
  3. Latin square design
  4. Factorial design
  Latin square design

Question 45 :
A Hypothesis contributes to the development of

  1. Theory
  2. Generalization

  4. Concept

Question 46 :
Which of the following sampling methods could be used to make an estimate of the sampling error?

  1. Convenience sampling
  2. Probability sampling
  3. Quota sampling
  4. Snow-ball sampling

Question 47 :
Basing conclusions without any bias and value judgment is ______.

  1. Objectivity
  2. Specificity
  3. Values
  4. Facts

Question 48 :
A researcher divides the populations into PG, graduates and (10+2) students and using the random digit table he selects some of them from each. This is technically called

  1. Stratified sampling
  2. Stratified random sampling
  3. Representative sampling,
  4. Systematic Sampling

Question 49 :
A research which follows case study method is called ___.

  1. Clinical or Diagnostic
  2. Causal
  3. Analytical
  4. Qualitative
  Clinical or Diagnostic

Question 50 :
Third step in problem formulation is

  1. Literature survey
  2. Broad problem statement
  3. Understanding the nature of problem
  4. Discussion

Question 51 :
Which type of sampling technique is used where People interviewed in a shopping center for their political opinion for a TV programme?

  1. Convenience sampling

  3. Multi-stage sampling

Question 60 :
Clinical or diagnostic research is

  1. The development of hypotheses rather than their testing
  2. A researcher is free to pick up a problem, redesign the enquiry as he proceeds and is prepared to conceptualize as he wishes.
  3. Follow case-study methods or in-depth approaches to reach the basic causal relations.
