Question 61 :
Second step in problem formulation is

  1. Statement of the problem
  2. Understanding the nature of the problem
  3. Survey
  4. Discussions
  Understanding the nature of the problem

Question 62 :
What type of editing implies that all forms should get a thorough editing by a single editor in a small study and by a team of editors in case of a large inquiry?

  1. Field
  2. Central
  3. Translating
  4. Simple
  Central

  1. Conventional
  2. Primary

  Primary

  1. 0 and 1

Question 69 :
The most frequently occurring score in a distribution

  1. Mean
  2. Mode
  3. Median

Question 70 :
A good qualitative problem statement:

  1. Defines the independent and dependent variables
  2. Conveys a sense of emerging design
  3. Specifies a research hypothesis to be tested
  4. Specifies the relationship between variables that the researcher expects to find

Question 71 :
In the determination of average per cent of change i.e., it is often used in the preparation of index numbers or when we deal in ratios, which type of mean is used?

  1. Geometric mean
  2. Harmonic mean
  3. Mode
  4. Median
  Geometric mean

Question 72 :
If we need to study the effect of two extraneous variables on dependent variable, which experimental research design should be used?

  1. Latin Square design
  2. Randomised block diagram
  3. Factorial design
  4. Completely randomised design
  Latin Square design

Question 73 :
Which of the following is not a problem statement for the applied research study

  1. Is genetically modified food hurting health?
  2. Is violence in the media and in video games damaging children's mental health?
  3. A study assessing whether men or women are more likely to suffer from depression
  4. How does immigration affect the economy of the India

Question 74 :
In an experimental hypothesis-testing research, when a group is exposed to usual conditions, it is called as _____.

  1. Control group
  2. Experimental group
  3. Usual group
  4. Treatment group
  Control group

Question 75 :
Social science research provides

  1. Research degree along with its consequential benefits
  2. Solution for an immediate problem
  3. Conclusions which are capable of being verified by observation or experiment

Question 84 :
Which of the following is not absolute meaning of Research methodology

Question 85 :
From a random sample of 36 New Delhi civil service personnel, the mean age and the sample standard deviation were found to be 40 years and 4.5 years respectively. Construct a 95 per cent confidence interval for the mean age of civil servants in New Delhi.

  1. 40±1.47
  2. 44.5±1.47
  3. 40±1.7
  4. 45±1.7
  40±1.47

Question 86 :
Which one of the following principles is not applicable to sampling?

  1. Sample units must be clearly defined
  2. Sample units must be dependent on each other
  3. Same units of sample should be used throughout the study
  4. Sample units must be chosen in a systematic and objective manner
